**"Tell me about a time you shipped a payments product 0 to 1."**

*Lead story — Mastercard PG launch at ICICI:*
- **S:** ICICI had no custom payment gateway — merchant payments ran through third-party TSPs with limited control over routing, pricing, and data.

### Audience: `peer-tech`

---

**"Walk me through how a cross-border payment actually works."**

*Strong answer structure:*
1. Merchant initiates payment → hits PSP (GlomoPay) API
2. PSP validates, applies AML/KYC screening
3. Routes through local payment rail (UPI/IMPS in India, SEPA in EU, ACH in US, etc.)
4. FX conversion at agreed rate (spot, forward, or mid-market)
5. Settlement: GlomoPay holds multi-currency accounts, nets positions
6. Funds disbursed to beneficiary account in destination currency
7. Reconciliation + reporting back to merchant

*Key risk points to mention:* FX volatility window, settlement failure/delay, AML screening false positives causing friction, regulatory hold requirements by jurisdiction.

---

**"How would you measure success for GlomoPay's payment gateway?"**

*North Star:* TPV (Total Payment Volume processed)

*Supporting metrics:*
- Merchant activation rate (onboarded → first transaction)
- Transaction success rate (by payment rail, by corridor)
- FX spread captured vs. mid-market (revenue quality)
- Time-to-onboard (days from signup → first live transaction)
- Merchant churn / reactivation rate
- AML false-positive rate (friction indicator)

*Why this matters for Glomo:* At seed stage, TPV growth and merchant retention are the two metrics that drive the next funding round.

---

**"How would you prioritize: FX optimization vs. AML automation vs. new payment rail?"**

*Framework answer:*
> "I'd start with the constraint that's most limiting TPV growth today. If merchants are dropping off at AML/KYC, automate that first — it's a leaky bucket. If they're converting fine but churning on FX rates, optimize FX. New rails expand TAM but require the most capital and compliance work — I'd validate demand via merchant research before committing. At Glomo's stage, I'd default to 'fix the existing funnel before expanding' — the seed-stage PM's job is to find the one thing that unlocks the next 10x, not spread resources across three initiatives."

---

**"What are the failure modes of a cross-border disbursement product?"**

- **FX rate lock failure:** Merchant sees one rate, settlement happens at a worse rate — trust destroyed.
- **Rail-level failures:** Local payment rail downtime (IMPS blackout, SWIFT delays) → manual fallback needed.
- **AML false positives:** Legitimate transactions frozen → merchant churn, SLA breach.
- **Settlement mismatch:** Multi-currency netting errors → financial liability.
- **Regulatory surprise:** IFSCA rule change or RBI circular that requires product changes mid-flight.
- **Reconciliation failures:** Merchant can't reconcile which payment maps to which invoice → support overload.

*From your ICICI experience:* "I've lived the UPI SMS notification failure problem — a 7.4% failure rate sounds small but it directly caused merchant churn because they couldn't confirm payment receipt. Reliability in payments is binary. Either it works every time, or it's broken."

---

**Reverse questions for the technical/peer panel**

1. "What's your current transaction success rate by corridor, and where are the biggest drop-offs?"
2. "How do you handle IFSCA reporting requirements today — manual or automated?"
3. "What does on-call look like for the payments infrastructure — how many incidents per week?"

- **What NOT to volunteer:** Hard comp number before you hear their band. Anxiety about cross-border experience gap — save that framing for the HM round where you can control it better.

### To the hiring manager (likely CEO Akash Arun)

- **What to lead with:** "I've shipped payment infrastructure at scale — here's the specific work, the metrics, and what I learned." Then pivot to why you're here: the GIFT City moat, the cross-border frontier, the startup velocity.
- **Vocabulary to use:** "merchant TPV", "payment rails", "regulatory moat", "0→1", "corridor" (for cross-border routes). Avoid saying "we" without context — at a seed company, the CEO knows every team member; say "I" with clear ownership.
- **Sharp angles:** GlomoPay's blog has a post on GIFT City strategy ([Glomo and GIFT City](https://blog.glomopay.com/glomo-collaborates-with-gift-city-the-evolution-and-strategic-leap-into-the-future-of-finance/)). Read it. Reference something specific from it in the conversation.
- **Bank→startup concern:** The CEO will have this concern. Address it before they ask. Frame ICICI as "the hardest environment to ship in — if I moved product there, I can move faster here."
- **Things to avoid:** Saying "at ICICI we did things a certain way." They don't care. Say "here's what I learned works."

---

### To the peer / technical panel

- **What to lead with:** Payment systems architecture knowledge. Show you understand the stack, not just the PM abstraction layer.
- **Things to avoid:** Vague metrics ("we grew revenue significantly"). Every claim needs a number.
- **Reverse questions:** On-call load, transaction success rates, current IFSCA compliance gaps they're working on.
- **Anti-pattern to avoid:** Don't over-rely on ICICI examples. Translate them. "At ICICI we had X problem; the equivalent at Glomo's scale is Y, and here's how I'd approach it."
